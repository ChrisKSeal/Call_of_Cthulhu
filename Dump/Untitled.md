DIARY - T.W. BRENNAN
Motor Mechanic, Whitechapel Road, London

October 8th, 1925
Finally saved enough for a proper walking holiday. Doctor says 
the country air will do me good - "help with the nerves," he 
says. Still wake up some nights thinking I hear the engines. 
Funny how motor cars sound like aeroplanes in the dark.

Caught the 8:15 to Swindon this morning. Countryside looks

October 9th

staring at my hands. Suppose they're still stained from 
the garage work.

Walked to the stone circle this afternoon. Magnificent sight,

Met another walker there - tall fellow with military bearing. 
Said his name was Thornfield. Seemed to know I'd served 
without me mentioning it.

October 10th
Strange dreams last night. Running through forests, but 
not afraid - excited, like hunting something. Woke with 
the taste of copper in my mouth.

Thornfield appeared at breakfast, invited me to join his 
"walking group." Says they know all the old paths, the 
ones not on the Ordnance Survey maps. There's something 
about his eyes when he talks about the countryside. 
Reminds me of how Sergeant Mills looked just before we 
went over the top.

The landlord's wife keeps crossing herself when she 
thinks I'm not looking.

October 11th
Went with Thornfield's group today. Seven of us in total - 
all men who'd served, though we didn't discuss the war 
much. They showed me a path through the woods that led 
to a clearing I swear wasn't on my map.

In the center stood the largest wicker construction I've 
ever seen - shaped like a stag, tall as a church steeple. 
Thornfield called it "the God's own cathedral." When I 
asked what god, he just smiled and said I'd understand soon.

One of the other walkers - Crawford, I think - whispered 
that it was "better than the trenches." I'm beginning 
to understand what he meant.

October 12th
Last entry. They've invited me to stay for the "celebration" 
on the equinox. Said I'm exactly the sort of hunter they've 
been looking for. The way Thornfield said "hunter" made 
something stir in my chest - something that's been sleeping 
since Passchendaele.

If someone finds this diary, know that I went willingly. 
The war took something from all of us, but maybe these 
people can give it back. Maybe we can be what we were 
meant to be, before civilization made us weak.

The old gods are calling. Time to answer.

- T.W.B.
