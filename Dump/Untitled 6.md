## **Scene 4B: The Puzzle Box Discovery**

**Setting:** Hidden among Whitmore's research materials or Blackwood's office

### Physical Description:

An ancient bronze cylinder, approximately 8 inches long and 3 inches in diameter. The surface is covered in concentric rings of Celtic knotwork, each ring containing letters of the Latin alphabet interspersed with ogham script symbols.

**Construction Details (Archaeology/Mechanical Repair):**

- Bronze shows green patina consistent with great age
- Rings rotate smoothly despite apparent antiquity
- Internal mechanism clicks softly as rings align
- Faint inscriptions in Latin around the base: "HEARNE DEUS SILVARUM" (Herne, God of the Forests)
- Weight suggests something substantial contained within

**Opening Mechanism (Intelligence/Mechanical Repair):**

- Six rotating rings must align to spell "HEARNE" using Latin letters
- Wrong combinations cause mild electric shock (1 point damage)
- Correct alignment produces audible click and cylinder opens lengthwise
- Internal mechanism is remarkably sophisticated for ancient artifact

### Contents Revealed:

**The Map Fragment:**

- Piece of ancient vellum showing portions of Britain and Ireland
- Locations marked with Celtic symbols correlating to modern Ordnance Survey maps
- Particular emphasis on stone circles, barrows, and "thin places"
- Maye's Howe clearly marked with elaborate decoration
- Route marked from various English locations converging on Orkney

**The Text Fragment:**

- Excerpt from Caleddin's Scroll written in Latin with Celtic annotations
- Translation reveals: "When Samhain moon rises full, the Horned One may walk among mortals again"
- Details about "willing sacrifice of ancient blood" and "convergence of ley lines"

**The Genealogical Chart:**

**The Warning:**
