#### **Item 1: The True Counter-Ritual**

**Physical Description:** Handwritten manuscript on expensive vellum, 15 pages  
**Language:** Latin with Celtic ogham annotations  
**Condition:** Some water damage, suggesting it was hidden outdoors before being placed in box

**Content Summary:**

```
RITUALE CONTRA CERNUNNOS
(Ritual Against Cernunnos)
Translated from the Chronicle of Brother Aldric, 
Glastonbury Abbey, 1347 CE

To bind the Horned One when he walks among mortals:

REQUIREMENTS:
- Seven silver arrows blessed at seven different churches
- Salt from the Dead Sea, mixed with ashes of rowan wood
- The blood of one who has resisted the Hunt's call
- A circle of cold iron, unbroken, surrounding the manifestation site
- The True Name spoken thrice in the old tongue

THE BINDING WORDS:
"Cernunnos Dubnorix, Lord of the Deep Forest,
By iron bound, by silver pierced, by salt contained,
Return to thy realm beyond the veil,
Until the wheel turns and seasons call thee home."

WARNING: The ritual must be completed before the god's 
physical manifestation solidifies. Once fully manifest 
in our world, only his willing departure or complete 
destruction of his earthly anchor can banish him.

CRITICAL NOTE: The "earthly anchor" is not the Horn alone, 
but the willing sacrifice of his chosen avatar. If Brother 
Thornfield completes his transformation, he becomes the 
permanent gateway for Cernunnos's power.
```

#### **Item 2: Family Confession Letter**

**Physical Description:** Personal stationary, Edmund Blackwood's letterhead  
**Writing:** Fountain pen, Edmund's handwriting  
**Date:** August 20th, 1925

**Content:**

```
My Dearest Nephew Reginald,

If you are reading this, then my worst fears have come to pass 
and I am no longer able to resist what our family has become. 
I had hoped that by rejecting the old ways and embracing 
scholarship, I could break the curse that has plagued the 
Blackwood line for centuries.

I was wrong.

The truth is that our ancestor, Lord Aldrich Blackwood, did not 
"found" the Antlered Brotherhood in 1889. He was recruited by 
forces far older and more terrible than any Victorian occult 
society. The Brotherhood has existed since Roman times, passing 
down through bloodlines, waiting for the proper celestial 
alignment.

Every generation, one Blackwood is chosen to serve. My father 
served. His father before him. Now you have been selected, and 
I fear I am too weak to stop you.

But know this: the god you think you serve cares nothing for 
humanity's spiritual enlightenment. Cernunnos is not a nature 
deity seeking balance. He is a force of primordial chaos who 
views civilization as an infection to be burned away. If you 
succeed in your ritual, you will not rule over a renewed world. 
You will preside over its death.

The counter-ritual I have hidden may be our family's only 
chance at redemption. Use it, nephew. Break the chain that 
has bound our bloodline to this ancient evil.

Your remorseful uncle,
Edmund Blackwood

P.S. - I have arranged for certain materials to be placed in 
the university's Restricted Collection. If the worst happens, 
perhaps others can succeed where I have failed.
```

### **Key Sections**

#### **Chapter III: The Roman Conquest and Celtic Resistance**

```
In the third year of Agricola's campaign against the Britons, 
the druids of Mona made their final stand against the eagles 
of Rome. As recorded by the centurion Marcus Flavius:

"The priests spoke words in their heathen tongue, and the very 
stones began to weep blood. The forest itself took arms against 
us, and beasts that walked like men came forth from the sacred 
groves. Our standardbearer swore he saw a figure with the head 
of a stag and antlers that scraped the clouds, but this I 
attribute to battle-madness.

When we finally took the island, we found circles of standing 
stones arranged in patterns that hurt the eyes to look upon. 
The druids had carved symbols into the rock that seemed to move 
when viewed by torchlight. General Agricola ordered all such 
stones thrown down and the symbols defaced, but even so, the 
men reported strange dreams and waking visions for months after."

[Marginal note in different hand: "The stones were not destroyed 
but buried. The symbols remain, waiting for those who know how 
to read them." - A.B., 1889]
```

#### **Chapter VII: The Medieval Manifestations**

```
Anno Domini 1347, during the great mortality [Black Death], 
Brother Thomas of Avebury reported the following:

"The sickness came not from corruption of the air, but from 
the awakening of things that should sleep. In the stone circles 
of our shire, we found evidence of heathen practices - circles 
of salt, bones arranged in patterns of power, and great wicker 
constructions in the shape of beasts.

The local lord, one William de Blackwood, was discovered leading 
these rites. When confronted, he claimed that only by feeding 
the old gods could the pestilence be turned aside. Seven souls 
he had already offered, and planned seven more, when divine 
providence brought his wickedness to light.

Yet I confess, after his execution, the mortality in our region 
did indeed cease, while neighboring shires continued to suffer. 
Whether this was God's mercy or the devil's bargain, I dare not 
speculate."

[Marginal note: "The Blackwood line survives. They learn. They 
adapt. They wait." - A.B., 1889]
```

#### **Chapter XII: The Elizabethan Incident**

```
From the private papers of Dr. John Dee, Astrologer Royal:

"Her Majesty has commanded me to investigate reports of 
supernatural occurrences in the Cotswold region. What I 
discovered there shakes my faith in both natural philosophy 
and divine providence.

A society of learned men, led by one Master Richard Blackwood, 
had assembled at the ancient stone circle near Avebury. Their 
purpose, as they explained it, was to commune with the spirits 
of Britain's ancient inhabitants for the good of the realm.

The ritual I witnessed defies all Christian understanding. 
Seven souls were offered to a being they called the Horned 
King, and in return, this entity promised to drive back the 
Spanish fleet then approaching our shores.

I am ashamed to record that the ritual appeared successful. 
Within days, storms scattered the Armada, and England was saved. 
Yet the cost... the screams of the sacrificed still echo in 
my dreams.

I have advised Her Majesty that this society must be disbanded, 
their practices forbidden. Yet I fear that powers once awakened 
are not easily put to sleep again."

[Marginal note: "Dee was a fool. The Hunt cannot be disbanded, 
only delayed. Each failure feeds the next attempt." - A.B., 1889]
```

#### **Chapter XVIII: The Counter-Rituals**

```
Brother Aldric writes:

"Having studied the accounts of previous manifestations, I have 
compiled what knowledge exists regarding the binding of the 
Horned One. The ancients knew that such powers could not be 
destroyed, only contained until their natural season passed.

The most reliable account comes from the Chronicle of Saint 
Dunstan, who faced a similar manifestation in 943:

REQUIREMENTS FOR BINDING:
1. The ritual circle must be broken by cold iron
2. Seven blessed weapons must pierce the manifestation
3. The true name must be spoken by one who has resisted the call
4. Salt from sacred ground must contain the essence
5. The willing avatar must be turned from his purpose

Note well: If the avatar's transformation completes, he becomes 
a permanent gateway. The god may then manifest at will, without 
need of ritual or sacrifice. This is the true danger - not a 
single manifestation, but eternal presence in our world.

The Blackwood bloodline carries within it the seed of this 
transformation. Each generation that serves strengthens the bond. 
Only by turning one of their own against the purpose can the 
chain be broken."

[Extensive marginal notes in multiple hands, ending with: 
"The chain will NOT be broken. What was begun in blood will 
be completed in fire." - R.B., 1925]
```

## **USING THESE MATERIALS**

### **Investigation Benefits**

**The Counter-Ritual Provides:**

- **Specific requirements** for stopping Cernunnos
- **Warning** about Thornfield's role as permanent gateway
- **Timeline pressure** - must act before transformation completes
- **Tactical intelligence** about the cult's true capabilities

**The Manuscript Offers:**

- **Historical context** showing this is part of centuries-long pattern
- **Family connections** revealing the conspiracy's scope
- **Previous failures** and why they occurred
- **Maps and diagrams** for tactical planning

### **Complications These Create**

**Access Challenges:**

- **Bank manager** suspicious of investigators' motives
- **Dr. Blackthorne** actively works to prevent manuscript access
- **Time pressure** - gathering materials takes precious hours
- **Cult awareness** - their agents monitor both locations

**Information Overload:**

- **Too much history** can overwhelm players
- **Multiple counter-rituals** with different requirements
- **Conflicting accounts** of what actually works
- **Moral complexity** - some "solutions" require terrible choices

### **Keeper's Notes**

**Pacing Suggestions:**

- **Reveal gradually** - don't dump all information at once
- **Require effort** - make players work to access and understand materials
- **Create choices** - force decisions about which information to prioritize
- **Time limits** - cult doesn't wait while investigators research

**Plot Integration:**

- **Safety deposit box** discovered mid-Act II after Professor Blackwood's true nature revealed
- **Manuscript** accessed during preparation for final confrontation
- **Both materials** essential for understanding how to stop the ritual
- **Missing elements** still require investigation and problem-solving

These materials provide investigators with the tools they need to potentially succeed, while creating new complications and time pressures. The historical depth adds weight to the threat while the specific counter-ritual requirements give players clear objectives for the final confrontation.
