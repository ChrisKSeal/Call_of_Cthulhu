### Scene 1: The Disappearance

**Setting:** London, early October 1925

**Opening Hook:**

- Investigators are contacted by the British Museum, Scotland Yard, or mutual acquaintances
- Dr. Margaret Whitmore, respected archaeologist, has been missing for three days
- Her flat shows signs of struggle - overturned furniture, broken pottery, strange symbols chalked on the walls
- Neighbors report hearing "foreign chanting" and seeing hooded figures near her building

**Key Clues:**

- Whitmore's research notes mention Celtic burial sites and "unusual patron interest"
- Letters from someone signing as "G.T." requesting private consultations
- Her uncle Professor Edmund Blackwood hasn't been seen since she disappeared
- A partially burned photograph showing Whitmore at an archaeological dig with unidentified aristocratic gentleman

### Scene 2: Academic Circles

**Setting:** British Museum, University College London

**Investigation Opportunities:**

- Interview Whitmore's colleagues about her recent behavior and research
- Discover her specialization in Romano-Celtic archaeology
- Learn about her family connection to Professor Blackwood
- Find references to Caleddin's Scroll in her personal notes
- Meet other academics who've had dealings with mysterious "G.T."

**Key NPCs:**

- Dr. Henry Pemberton (colleague, concerned about Whitmore's recent obsession with "pre-Roman mysteries")
- Museum security guard who saw "well-dressed gentleman" asking about Celtic artifacts
- Librarian who remembers Whitmore researching "unusual patron requests"

### Scene 3: The Return

**Setting:** Whitmore's flat or hospital, three days after disappearance

**The Revelation:**

- Whitmore returns, found wandering in Hyde Park at dawn
- She's disheveled, confused, with no memory of the past three days
- Claims she was "visiting family in the countryside" but details don't match
- Occasionally slips into speaking ancient Brythonic words
- Shows knowledge of Celtic rituals she previously didn't possess

**Investigative Challenges:**

- Whitmore seems helpful but something is "off" about her
- She unconsciously traces Celtic symbols while talking
- Shows aversion to Christian symbols or holy ground
- Medical examination reveals she's been drugged with unknown herbal compounds

### Scene 4: First Contact with the Supernatural

**Setting:** Following Whitmore or investigating her "countryside" claims

**Supernatural Encounters:**

- Shadow hounds track the investigators
- Strange dreams plague anyone who spends time near Whitmore
- Celtic symbols appear burned into grass or stone where she's walked
- Witnesses report seeing a "wild hunt" on foggy nights

**Key Discovery:**

- The bronze puzzle box, found hidden in Whitmore's research materials
- Opens when "Hearne" is entered on the rotating letter rings
- Contains fragment of ancient text and map showing locations across Britain

## **ACT II: UNRAVELING THE CONSPIRACY**

_"The web of ancient evil spreads wider than imagined..."_

### Scene 5: The Blackwood Connection

**Setting:** Professor Blackwood's estate, countryside manor

**Meeting the "Ally":**

- Professor Edmund Blackwood appears as concerned uncle and helpful expert
- Offers to assist with translating ancient texts and understanding Celtic lore
- Provides seemingly helpful information about druids and Roman persecution
- Subtly guides investigation toward specific locations and conclusions

**Hidden Agenda:**

- Blackwood is possessed but fighting for control
- Occasionally makes mistakes that reveal supernatural knowledge
- Steers investigators away from discovering Thornfield's true plans
- Plants false clues to misdirect the investigation

**Clues to His True Nature:**

- Servants whisper about his "changed behavior" since last Halloween
- He knows more about recent events than he should
- Shows unusual interest in investigators' findings about Whitmore
- Library contains books on Celtic possession and binding rituals

### Scene 6: The Cult's Reach

**Setting:** Various locations across England and Scotland

**Expanding Investigation:**

- Following leads from the puzzle box and Blackwood's "guidance"
- Discovering other missing persons with Celtic ancestry
- Encountering Harold Morrison and witnessing his "Warp Spasm" transformation
- Learning about Lord Gareth Thornfield through society connections

**Key Encounters:**

- **Morrison's Attack:** Shell-shocked ex-Captain transforms into bestial creature during combat
- **Society Contacts:** Upper-class NPCs who know Thornfield as eccentric antiquarian
- **Missing Persons:** Pattern of disappearances among families with Celtic bloodlines
- **Archaeological Sites:** Ancient locations showing signs of recent ritual activity

### Scene 7: The Horrific Discovery

**Setting:** Hidden cult location (abandoned church, ancient stone circle, or remote estate)

**The Grisly Find:**

- Investigators discover the preserved body of Reginald Elwood
- Corpse is drained of blood, packed in salt and herbs, placed in massive Celtic cauldron
- Ancient symbols carved into surrounding stones
- Evidence of ritual preparation for reanimation at next full moon

**Escalating Horror:**

- Whitmore's behavior becomes increasingly erratic
- She speaks in Moiren's voice, revealing hatred for "Roman oppressors"
- Shadow hounds become more aggressive and numerous
- Other cult members begin hunting the investigators

### Scene 8: The Betrayal Revealed

**Setting:** Confrontation with Blackwood or discovery of his true nature

**The Truth Emerges:**

- Evidence mounts that Blackwood has been manipulating the investigation
- His possession is revealed through:
    - Speaking in ancient Celtic languages
    - Supernatural knowledge of investigator actions
    - Violent reaction to Christian symbols
    - Moments where the possessing spirit takes full control

**The Real Plan:**

- Blackwood reveals (willingly or unwillingly) the true scope of Thornfield's scheme
- Samhain ceremony at Maye's Howe to summon Cernunnos avatar
- Whitmore/Moiren's willing sacrifice in the wicker man
- The cult's goal to restore "the old ways" and destroy Christian civilization

**Cliffhanger:**

- Whitmore disappears again, taken north to Orkney
- Investigators realize they have only days before Samhain
- The full moon approaches, threatening Elwood's reanimation

## **ACT III: THE SAMHAIN HORROR**

_"On the night when the veil grows thin, ancient gods demand their due..."_

### Scene 9: Race to Orkney

**Setting:** Journey to the Orkney Islands, Scotland

**The Desperate Chase:**

- Investigators must reach Maye's Howe before the Samhain ceremony
- Travel complications: storms, mechanical failures, cult interference
- Encounters with shadow hounds and wild hunt spirits
- Growing supernatural phenomena as Samhain approaches

**Gathering Allies:**

- Potential rescue of other intended victims
- Recruiting local help (though many locals are either cult sympathizers or too terrified to help)
- Acquiring tools and weapons for the final confrontation
- Learning last-minute information about stopping the ritual

### Scene 10: The Ancient Tomb

**Setting:** Maye's Howe, Samhain night (October 31st)

**Infiltrating the Ceremony:**

- The neolithic tomb is surrounded by cultists and supernatural guardians
- Massive wicker man constructed nearby, with Whitmore trapped inside
- Thornfield conducts the ritual using Caleddin's Scroll
- Moiren's spirit grows stronger, overwhelming Whitmore's consciousness

**Environmental Challenges:**

- Howling winds and supernatural storm
- Shadow hounds prowling the area
- Ancient spirits rising from the tomb
- Reality becoming unstable as the ritual progresses

### Scene 11: The Final Ritual

**Setting:** Inside and around Maye's Howe

**The Climactic Confrontation:**

- Cultists defend the ceremony with fanatical devotion
- Harold Morrison undergoes complete Warp Spasm transformation
- Blackwood's possessed spirit fights for control, potentially aiding or hindering investigators
- Moiren/Whitmore prepares for willing self-sacrifice

**Supernatural Escalation:**

- Cernunnos begins manifesting as reality weakens
- The Wild Hunt materializes around the tomb
- Ancient Celtic spirits possess cultists and investigators alike
- Ley line energies create unpredictable magical effects

### Scene 12: Gods and Monsters

**Setting:** The burning wicker man and ritual climax

**The Final Choice:**

- Investigators must choose between saving Whitmore and stopping the ritual
- Moiren's spirit is willing to die to complete the summoning
- Whitmore fights for moments of control, begging for help or death
- The wicker man burns as Cernunnos's avatar begins to manifest

**Possible Outcomes:**

**Success:**

- Investigators disrupt the ritual before completion
- Whitmore is saved (though possibly permanently changed)
- Cernunnos's manifestation fails or is banished
- Surviving cultists flee or are captured

**Partial Success:**

- The ritual is stopped but at great cost
- Whitmore dies but prevents the summoning
- Some investigators are possessed or driven insane
- Cernunnos manifests briefly before being banished

**Failure:**

- The Horned God's avatar walks the earth
- Whitmore dies willingly in the flames
- Ancient Celtic spirits begin possessing people across Britain
- The investigators must flee or join the fight against spreading supernatural chaos

**Epilogue Possibilities:**

- Government cover-up of supernatural events
- Investigators dealing with trauma and supernatural after-effects
- Surviving cult members plotting revenge or new rituals
- The long-term consequences of awakened ancient powers

---

## **Pacing Notes:**

**Act I** (2-3 sessions): Focus on mystery and investigation, building atmosphere  
**Act II** (2-3 sessions): Revelation and horror escalation, supernatural encounters increase  
**Act III** (1-2 sessions): Action and climax, full supernatural horror unleashed

**Total Runtime:** 5-8 sessions depending on group investigation style and Keeper pacing preferences.
