# **"The Horn of the Wild Hunt" - HANDOUT TIMING GUIDE**

_When to Reveal Each Clue for Maximum Impact_

---

## **ACT I: MUSEUM MYSTERIES - HANDOUTS 1-6**

### **HANDOUT #1: DR. WHITMORE'S RESEARCH NOTES**

**When:** First investigation of her office at the British Museum  
**Dramatic Timing:** After investigators have examined the Horn but before they understand its significance  
**Purpose:** Establishes the central mystery and gives first clues about the Brotherhood  
**Keeper Notes:** Hide this well - make them search her desk thoroughly. The urgency in her final note should create immediate tension.

### **HANDOUT #2: NEWSPAPER CLIPPING**

**When:** Found tucked inside Handout #1, or discovered during library research  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators need direction about where to go next  
**Purpose:** Provides geographic focus and establishes pattern of disappearances  
**Keeper Notes:** Can also be "discovered" by having an NPC (Professor Blackwood) bring it to their attention if they're stuck.

### **HANDOUT #3: POLICE MISSING PERSONS REPORT**

**When:** Obtained from Detective Inspector Morrison, or found at the Red Lion pub  
**Dramatic Timing:** Early in Act II when investigators arrive in Avebury  
**Purpose:** Gives concrete details about victims and establishes police incompetence  
**Keeper Notes:** Morrison should be reluctant to share this - requires social skills or leverage to obtain.

### **HANDOUT #4: OGHAM TRANSLATION CHART**

**When:** Found with Dr. Whitmore's research materials, or obtained from Professor Blackwood  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators encounter Ogham inscriptions they can't read  
**Purpose:** Allows investigators to decode inscriptions themselves (player engagement)  
**Keeper Notes:** Don't give this too early - let them struggle with the symbols first to build mystery.

### **HANDOUT #5: ASTRONOMICAL CHARTS**

**When:** Discovered during research into the ritual timing  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators realize there's a specific deadline approaching  
**Purpose:** Creates urgency and reveals the 400-year cycle  
**Keeper Notes:** Best delivered by Professor Blackwood or found in university archives. The astronomer's personal warnings add credibility.

### **HANDOUT #6: BROTHER ASHWOOD'S CONSTRUCTION SKETCHES**

**When:** Found at Blackwood Estate during Act II investigation  
**Dramatic Timing:** Just before investigators discover the actual Wicker Stag  
**Purpose:** Prepares them for the horror while providing tactical information  
**Keeper Notes:** These should be blood-stained and disturbing. Ashwood's artistic notes about "living cathedral" should unsettle players.

## **ACT II: THE MODERN DRUIDS - HANDOUTS 7-13**

### **HANDOUT #7: PRISONER SCHEDULE**

**When:** Found at Blackwood Estate, hidden in cult meeting room  
**Dramatic Timing:** After investigators realize people are being held captive  
**Purpose:** Humanizes the victims and shows cult's methodical approach  
**Keeper Notes:** The clinical nature of the schedule should be chilling. Individual notes about prisoners create emotional investment.

### **HANDOUT #8: ANCIENT DRUIDIC TEXT**

**When:** Discovered in estate library or Thornfield's personal study  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators need to understand the ritual requirements  
**Purpose:** Provides key information about the "seven pure hearts" requirement  
**Keeper Notes:** The burned edges suggest someone tried to destroy it. Latin mixed with Ogham requires both Handout #4 and Language skills.

### **HANDOUT #9: THOMAS BRENNAN'S DIARY**

**When:** Found at the Red Lion pub or in his abandoned belongings  
**Dramatic Timing:** Early in Act II to show how recruitment works  
**Purpose:** Shows the seductive nature of cult recruitment and victim's willing participation  
**Keeper Notes:** The progression from skepticism to acceptance should be disturbing. His final sketch provides tactical intelligence.

### **HANDOUT #10: CULT RECRUITMENT LETTER**

**When:** Found among Thornfield's correspondence at the estate  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators need to understand the cult's appeal  
**Purpose:** Reveals post-war context and Thornfield's military background  
**Keeper Notes:** Lord Blackwood's elegant prose contrasts with the horrific purpose. The postscript from "M.C." adds personal touch.

### **HANDOUT #11: MEDICAL REPORT**

**When:** Obtained through police contacts or found in Thornfield's belongings  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators wonder about Thornfield's background  
**Purpose:** Explains his transformation and provides psychological insight  
**Keeper Notes:** Dr. Rivers' growing concern throughout the report builds tension. The final warning should feel prophetic.

### **HANDOUT #12: ASTRONOMICAL CALCULATION SHEET**

**When:** Found at the estate or obtained from Royal Observatory  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators need precise timing for the ritual  
**Purpose:** Confirms the deadline and adds scientific credibility  
**Keeper Notes:** The astronomer's personal notes about being "troubled" add human element to technical data.

### **HANDOUT #13: POLICE INCIDENT REPORT**

**When:** Given by local constable or found in police files  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators need confirmation of cult activity  
**Purpose:** Provides official verification and local perspective  
**Keeper Notes:** Constable Williams' practical observations contrast with supernatural reality. His handwritten addition shows personal concern.

## **ACT III: THE WILD HUNT RISES - HANDOUTS 14-20**

### **HANDOUT #14: TELEGRAPH MESSAGES**

**When:** Found at various locations throughout Act II and early Act III  
**Dramatic Timing:** Reveal gradually to show escalating crisis  
**Purpose:** Shows communication network and builds timeline pressure  
**Keeper Notes:**

- **Telegram #1:** Found early, establishes Dr. Whitmore's desperation
- **Telegram #2:** Police station, shows official response
- **Telegram #3:** Estate, reveals cult coordination (coded language)
- **Telegram #4:** Police follow-up
- **Telegram #5:** Damaged, found at climax location

### **HANDOUT #15: WAR OFFICE CORRESPONDENCE**

**When:** Obtained through official channels or found in medical files  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators need to understand Thornfield's transformation  
**Purpose:** Provides military context and explains his "pack" leadership  
**Keeper Notes:** The witness statements from Canadian soldiers are crucial - they saw the change beginning. Colonel Pemberton's final note adds personal dread.

### **HANDOUT #16: UNIVERSITY DISCIPLINARY LETTER**

**When:** Found among Dr. Whitmore's personal effects or delivered to investigators  
**Dramatic Timing:** When players need to understand academic pressures she faced  
**Purpose:** Shows institutional pressure and gender discrimination context  
**Keeper Notes:** The pompous tone contrasts with the supernatural reality. Vice-Chancellor's threats seem petty compared to actual danger.

### **HANDOUT #17: BANK TRANSACTION RECORDS**

**When:** Obtained through police investigation or financial contacts  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators need to trace cult funding and connections  
**Purpose:** Shows cult's financial network and modern organization  
**Keeper Notes:** The amounts and timing reveal planning depth. Manager's notes show legitimate concern about suspicious activity.

### **HANDOUT #18: TRAIN TIMETABLE**

**When:** Given when investigators plan travel, or found at railway station  
**Dramatic Timing:** When transportation becomes crucial to timing  
**Purpose:** Shows travel limitations and creates logistical pressure  
**Keeper Notes:** Limited service forces investigators to plan carefully. Sunday restrictions could strand them at crucial moment.

### **HANDOUT #19: VILLAGE CENSUS RECORDS**

**When:** Found at local government office or church records  
**Dramatic Timing:** When investigators research local history and population  
**Purpose:** Shows pattern of disappearances and cult's long-term presence  
**Keeper Notes:** Enumerator Hartwell's suspicious death after filing report adds sinister element. Population decline shows cult's impact.

### **HANDOUT #20: ARCHAEOLOGICAL SURVEY MAP**

**When:** Found among Professor Blackwood's research or at the university  
**Dramatic Timing:** Before final confrontation, when investigators need tactical information  
**Purpose:** Provides detailed layout of ritual site and reveals underground passages  
**Keeper Notes:** Dr. Blackwood's growing concern throughout his notes builds tension. The final red ink additions show immediate danger.

## **STRATEGIC TIMING PRINCIPLES**

### **Information Cascade**

**Early Handouts (1-6):** Build mystery and establish direction  
**Middle Handouts (7-13):** Reveal scope and urgency  
**Late Handouts (14-20):** Provide tactical information and final context

### **Pacing Guidelines**

**Don't Front-Load:** Spread handouts across sessions to maintain discovery excitement  
**Reward Investigation:** Make handouts rewards for thorough searching and good roleplay  
**Create Urgency:** Later handouts should emphasize time pressure and escalating stakes  
**Allow Processing:** Give players time to discuss and analyze each handout

### **Backup Timing**

**If Players Get Stuck:**

- Handout #2 can be delivered by helpful NPC
- Handout #14 (telegrams) can arrive unexpectedly
- Handout #20 (map) can be "discovered" by Professor Blackwood

**If Players Rush Ahead:**

- Handouts #7-9 provide crucial tactical information they'll need
- Handout #15 explains enemy capabilities
- Handout #17 shows cult's resources and planning

### **Dramatic Reveals**

**Session Cliffhangers:**

- End Session 1 with Handout #6 (Wicker Stag sketches)
- End Session 2 with Handout #11 (Thornfield's medical report)
- End Session 3 with Handout #20 (survey map showing underground passages)

**Combat Preparation:**

- Give Handout #20 before final battle for tactical planning
- Reveal Handout #15 before confronting Thornfield directly
- Share Handout #17 if they need to understand cult's resources

## **KEEPER TIPS FOR HANDOUT DELIVERY**

### **Physical Presentation**

**Make Them Work:** Don't just hand over documents - make players search, ask questions, and solve puzzles  
**Environmental Storytelling:** Place handouts where they logically belong (desk drawers, filing cabinets, personal belongings)  
**Condition Matters:** Bloodstained, water-damaged, or partially burned handouts tell their own stories

### **Information Control**

**Partial Reveals:** Some handouts can be discovered in pieces across multiple sessions  
**Translation Challenges:** Require appropriate skills or NPCs to fully understand some documents  
**Missing Pieces:** Key information might be deliberately obscured or damaged

### **Player Engagement**

**Let Them Decode:** Handouts #4 and #8 allow players to translate inscriptions themselves  
**Discussion Time:** After major handouts, give players time to theorize and plan  
**Cross-Reference:** Reward players who connect information across multiple handouts

The key is to treat each handout as a story beat that advances the narrative while providing the info
