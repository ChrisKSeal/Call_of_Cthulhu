THE TRUSTEES OF THE BRITISH MUSEUM
do cordially request the honour of your presence at the

OPENING OF OUR NEWEST EXHIBITION 
"Sacred Mysteries of Ancient Albion"

Friday Evening, September 18th, 1926  
Eight o'clock

The Great Court & Egyptian Gallery 
The British Museum, Great Russell Street, London

Our distinguihed guests are invited to witness the unveiling of extraordinary artifacts recently excavated from sacred sites across Britain, including the magnificent Cauldron of Dagda.

Miss Gwenevar Cadwallon, a learned Celtic scholar and the exhibition's curator, will also present her groundbreaking research on the spiritual practices and cultural heritage of pre-Roman Britain.

The evening will feature:

Guided tours of the complete collection
Expert commentary on ritual significance and historical context
Light refreshments and champagne reception
Private viewing before public opening

Evening attire requested.

"In these ancient vessels lie the sacred mysteries of our ancestors, waiting to whisper their secrets to those who would listen."

Dr. Edmund Hartwell, Chief Curator
    

R.S.V.P. to the Museum Secretary by September 15th
