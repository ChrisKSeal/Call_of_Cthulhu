##  Name & Appearance

**Aldric Thornfield** stands tall despite his hunched, increasingly animalistic posture. _True antlers_—not a crown—now grow from his skull, branching like ancient oak, dried blood crusting their base. His once-refined features have coarsened; cheekbones sharpen, teeth elongate, fingernails blacken into claw-like points. He wears tattered Great War officer's uniform beneath ritual robes stained with earth and sacrifice.

##  Personality Traits

**Eloquent yet erratic**, Aldric shifts between cultured Oxford diction and guttural snarls mid-sentence. He possesses _unsettling charisma_ that draws followers despite his deterioration. His habit of tilting his head like a predator sensing prey unnerves even devoted cultists. He increasingly speaks of himself in the plural—"_we_ shall return."

##  Occupation & Role

Former British Army Captain and professor of Celtic archaeology at Oxford, now self-proclaimed High Priest of Cernunnos. His followers view him as a living saint destined for apotheosis; outsiders see only a dangerous madman leading a death cult through the Scottish Highlands.

##  Motivation & Goal

Aldric seeks **complete union with Cernunnos**, believing himself chosen to become the Horned God's mortal vessel and herald the return of the Old Ways. The trenches of the Somme broke something in him—watching thousands die in mechanized slaughter convinced him that _modern civilization is a cancer_ that must be purged. He discovered ancient texts suggesting the Celts' true power came from willing sacrifice and communion with primal deities. **His ultimate goal**: to complete the ritual requiring thirteen willing sacrifices, allowing Cernunnos to fully manifest in his transformed body and unleash supernatural vengeance upon the descendants of Rome—namely, all of Christian Europe. He believes this will restore balance to a world poisoned by industrialization and hollow faith.

##  Secret or Hook

Aldric's transformation is **irreversible and accelerating beyond his control**. While he preaches of glorious union, private moments reveal his terror—he weeps as his humanity slips away, as memories of his wife and daughter (killed in the 1918 flu) become alien and incomprehensible to his changing mind. His journal, hidden in his chambers, documents this deterioration and contains the _ritual's true final requirement_: the high priest's consciousness must be completely consumed, leaving only Cernunnos behind. **The tragic hook**: Aldric's daughter survived the flu and has been searching for her father for five years, arriving in the Highlands just as the final ritual approaches. She may be the only person who can reach whatever humanity remains—or become the thirteenth sacrifice he needs.
