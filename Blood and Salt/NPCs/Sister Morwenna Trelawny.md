##  Name & Appearance

**Sister Morwenna Trelawny** appears skeletal, her graying blonde hair cropped short in a manner both monastic and militaristic. She wears a patchwork of her former Anglican habit beneath rough woolen robes adorned with Celtic knotwork. _Her left hand trembles constantly_—a remnant of shell-shock—and her pale blue eyes rarely blink, giving her an unsettling, thousand-yard stare.

## Personality Traits

**Morwenna speaks in hushed, reverent tones**, as though constantly in prayer, but her words carry an _intensity that borders on mania_. She possesses an unnerving stillness broken by sudden, jerky movements. **Her habit**: touching her throat before speaking, checking for a cross that's no longer there, a phantom gesture from her former life.

## Occupation & Role

Spiritual medium and oracle for the Children of the Stag cult, serving as the primary conduit for Branwen's communications from beyond. Revered by cultists as the Horned God's prophet, feared by outsiders as a madwoman.

## Motivation & Goal

**Morwenna seeks absolution**—not from the Christian God she abandoned, but from the _blood-soaked memories_ of Passchendaele where she served as a volunteer nurse. She witnessed men dying while crying for mothers, for gods, for anyone who might hear. When Christianity offered only platitudes, **the certainty of Cernunnos** offered something she desperately craved: _a god who acknowledged death's reality and promised purpose through sacrifice_. She believes that by facilitating the Horned God's return, she'll prove those deaths in the trenches weren't meaningless—that the old gods see suffering where the new one looked away. **Every séance, every channeling of Branwen, brings her closer to the peace she lost in Flanders' mud.**

## Secret or Hook

**Morwenna isn't merely channeling Branwen**—_she's slowly being consumed by the ancient druidess_. Each trance leaves Branwen's consciousness lingering longer, overlaying Morwenna's memories with visions of Roman legions burning Ynys Môn. She's begun experiencing **temporal displacement**: speaking ancient Brythonic without realizing it, recoiling from iron as though burned, forgetting entire decades of her own life. The cult leadership knows but conceals this truth—they need her functional until the final ritual. Hidden in her former cell at the abandoned convent where she lived, investigators might find her _wartime journal_, documenting her crisis of faith and revealing the **names and locations of three other nurses** who served with her—women the cult plans to recruit or silence, as they alone know Morwenna's pre-cult identity and whereabouts of her surviving family.
