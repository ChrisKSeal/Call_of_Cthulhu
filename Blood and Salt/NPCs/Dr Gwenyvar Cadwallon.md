## Appearance

Gwen is a woman in her early thirties with auburn hair often pulled back in a practical bun and intelligent green eyes behind wire-rimmed spectacles. She dresses conservatively in wool skirts and cardigans appropriate for a scholarly woman of the 1920s. When possessed by [[Branwen]], her eyes take on an otherworldly gleam and she moves with an ancient, predatory grace that seems foreign to her normally bookish demeanor.

## Personality

**Pre-Possession:** Gwen is passionate about her work, particularly the preservation and interpretation of Celtic artifacts. She's methodical and precise, with a dry sense of humor and genuine enthusiasm for sharing knowledge. She's somewhat naive about the supernatural aspects of her research, viewing them through an academic lens rather than believing in their literal truth.

**Post-Possession:** When [[Branwen]] takes control, Gwen's demeanor shifts dramatically. She speaks with archaic authority, mixing modern English with Brythonic and Latin phrases. Her academic curiosity is replaced by cold fury toward Roman descendants and a terrible certainty about the righteousness of her cause. However, traces of Gwen's compassion occasionally surface, creating internal conflict.

## Backstory

Born in Wales, Gwen excelled in ancient history and languages at Oxford before joining the British Museum's Celtic collection. Her family tree traces back to a Roman centurion named Marcus Cadwallon who settled in Wales after marrying a local woman - making Gwen a descendant of both conquerors and conquered. This mixed heritage makes her the perfect vessel for [[Branwen|Branwen's]] return and the ideal willing sacrifice for [[Cernunnos]].
