## ACT I - A shadow grows

### Scene I.I - Shadow hound attacks in the underground. 

Thomas Hartwell, age 45 and a solicitor is slain by the hound. There is al ink to the Hartwell family that can be found in Gwen's research notes. Hartwell has a letter to discuss with Gwen, they are distantly related and have a connection to Plas Glynllifon, recently vacant due to the death of the 5th Baron Newborough who died intestate. Investigators are held up by Constable Roger McCormick until the area has been properly inspected, but are allowed to progress as it appears to be death by natural causes.

Clues: Copies of a medieval charter and of a deed of transfer under duress from 1649, verified by the chief librarian of the National Library of Wales. Handwritten note exploring the familial relationship between Thomas and Gwenyvar Cadwallon (they are 5th cousins) with referenced documents.

### Scene I.II - Arrival at the Museum

The investigators arrive to find the Police present at the Museum. Detective Inspector William Hayes is in charge of the scene and apologises to the investigators. It seems that tonight's event has had to be called off due to the theft of the exhibit's crown jewel, the Cauldron of Dagda. Gwenyvar will barge into the middle of the conversation demanding to know what the police are doing to retrieve the cauldron. She refers to "barbarians" and "the container of souls". Investigators making a Hard Psychology check will recognise clues that she is not as angry as she appears to be. Her demeanor abruptly changes and she is apologetic for her outburst, seemingly confused as to why she is here. She will excuse herself, making some comment about being overwrought from the loss and head back to her office.

Clues: Gwen's change of behaviour is Branwen losing control momentarily. She has hazy recollections of why she is there and of her behaviour and is frightened that she is losing her mind. If asked about the "container of souls", she has no idea why she called it that.

### Scene I.III - Gwen's office

Gwen has a postdoctoral fellow from Imperial College who is working for her, Edward Collingwood. Edward has a desk in the antechamber to Gwen's office and will advise the investigators not to disturb her. If pressed he will say that she has developed a nasty temper in the last few weeks and has begun to curse him in Welsh, a language he didn't realise she spoke. He doesn't speak Welsh and is assuming that that is the language she is speaking, when in reality it is Brythonic. In her office Gwen is appearing confused and contrary to the warning the investigators have heard, she is considerate and prepared to talk to them. About 10 minutes into the conversation she will straighten up and grunt as if struck before standing and telling the investigators that she must attend other matters and that they are free to go. Once they leave she will head out shortly after if the characters wish to return to her office.

Clues: Eagle eyed characters making a Spot Hidden roll will catch a glimpse of her notes including the linguistic shift Caecilius to Caecilus to Caedwaladr to ap Cadwaladr to ap Cadwalader to Cadwaladron to Cadwallon with handwritten notes <- evidence of her Roman heritage. This will be found if the characters explore her office with her not present.
Also in her office are research notes that include an extended genealogy linking Gwenyvar to the Hartwell's and to the Whitmore's. There are research notes about her recent finds with different handwriting that is scornful of the work that Gwen has written, evidence of Branwen. Of note are detailed ritual notes describing how the cauldron should be filled with blood drained from the sacrifice, mixed with salt and buried with the body. Luck rolls or Gwen returns while they are investigating. Branwen throws spells in this case, eyes rolling back into her head and Celtic runes appearing all over her face. She casts Breath of the Deep on the nearest investigator, before turning and running out of the room as Gwen reclaims her body. In any case Gwen is notably absent from now until Act II, having fled to Ynys Mons.
