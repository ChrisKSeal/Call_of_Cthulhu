
**Setting**: British Museum, Department of Celtic Antiquities, October 15th, 1925, late afternoon

### Opening Hook

Dr. James Harrington, a nervous man in his fifties with ink-stained fingers, approaches the investigators in the museum's Great Court. His scholarly composure is clearly shaken, and he clutches a telegram with trembling hands.

_"Thank heavens you've come. It's about my colleague, Dr. Margaret Whitmore. She's vanished without explanation, and I fear something dreadful has happened. The police dismiss it as a woman's hysteria, but Margaret would never abandon her life's work - especially not with Samhain approaching and her research so close to completion."_

### Dr. Whitmore's Office Investigation

The office is in disarray, clearly indicating a hurried departure rather than planned absence. Her teacup sits cold and half-empty, a half-eaten sandwich grows stale on a plate. The window stands open despite the October chill, and research papers are scattered across every surface.

**Atmosphere**: The room feels unnaturally cold, and shadows seem to linger longer than they should. Staff members whisper about strange sounds from the basement levels at night.

### Key Discoveries

**1. Research Materials**  
Scattered notes reveal Whitmore's obsession with Celtic bloodlines, particularly families with unbroken lineage to pre-Roman Britain. Her research focuses on:

- "Hearne's Sacred Groves" and references to the "Antlered One's Return"
- Genealogical charts tracing ancient druidic families
- Translation attempts of Ogham script with phrases like "When Samhain's moon rises..." and "...the blood-price opens the door..."

**2. The Puzzle Box**  
Hidden in a secret compartment within her desk drawer (**Spot Hidden** or **Library Use** to discover), investigators find an ancient bronze puzzle box. Seven rotating rings bear runic letters that can be arranged to spell words. The box feels unnaturally warm and seems to emit a barely perceptible humming sound.

**3. Personal Journal** (Final Entry, dated three days ago)  
_"The dreams grow stronger each night. I hear the hunting horns in the distance, and the shadow hounds bay for blood. The family curse is real - I understand that now. They're coming for me, just as they came for grandfather during the last cycle. But perhaps there's still time to break what's been set in motion. The old ways demand blood, but the old ways can be stopped. I must reach Thornborough before they find me. If I fail, may God forgive us all for what will be unleashed."_

## Three Clues Pointing to the Restricted Basement

### Clue 1: The Missing Artifact Requisition

**Discovery**: Found in Whitmore's desk, a requisition form dated one week ago requesting after-hours access to "Celtic Ritual Implements - Storage Level B3, Section 7." The form shows her authorization to examine the "Cauldron of Dagda - Catalog #CR-1847" for "comparative mythological research."

**Investigator Insight**: A **Library Use** or **Archaeology** roll reveals this cauldron is one of the legendary Thirteen Treasures of Britain, supposedly used in ancient druidic resurrection rituals. The storage location is deep in the museum's restricted basement archives.

### Clue 2: Security Guard's Strange Behavior

**Discovery**: Speaking with day security reveals that Albert Thornfield, the night security guard, has been acting increasingly erratic. His logbook shows multiple unauthorized visits to basement storage areas over the past month, always between midnight and 3 AM. Recent entries are signed with strange symbols instead of his signature.

**Investigator Insight**: A **Psychology** roll suggests severe mental disturbance, while **Anthropology** or **Occult** identifies the symbols as Ogham script. The day supervisor mentions Thornfield has been "muttering in some foreign tongue" and was once found kneeling before the Celtic stone circle exhibit, "whispering prayers to something he called the Horned God."

### Clue 3: Dr. Carstairs' Appointment Calendar

**Discovery**: On Whitmore's desk lies an appointment book belonging to Dr. Edwin Carstairs, the museum's Celtic artifacts specialist. The final entry, dated yesterday, reads: "11:30 PM - M.W. - Final examination of the Cauldron. Confirm bloodline compatibility. Storage B3-7. God help us all."

**Investigator Insight**: A **Spot Hidden** roll notices the handwriting becomes increasingly erratic in recent entries, with Ogham symbols appearing in the margins. The late-night timing and religious plea suggest something far more serious than academic research.

## Additional Clues for Act II (Thornborough)

**Family Genealogy Charts**: Multiple family trees pinned to a cork board, all tracing back to ancient Celtic regions. The Whitmore family tree shows an unbroken line stretching back over a thousand years, with notations about "Hearne's chosen bloodline" and "the thirteen-year cycle."

**Thornborough Research**: Maps and archaeological surveys of the Thornborough Henges, with notes about "ley line convergence" and "ritual alignment during Samhain." Whitmore has marked specific locations around the henges with Ogham symbols.

**Local Folklore Collection**: Books on Yorkshire legends, with passages highlighted about "Hearne's Hunt" and families "marked by the Wild God." References to cyclical disappearances every thirteen years around Samhain.

---

## Clues for the Ultimate Climax

**Orkney Connection**: Research materials include detailed studies of Maeshowe's astronomical alignments, with calculations showing how the winter solstice light penetrates the burial chamber. Notes suggest this alignment can be "reversed" during Samhain to open pathways between worlds.

**The Avatar Prophecy**: Translated Ogham texts speak of the "Great Working" - a ritual requiring the blood of thirteen generations poured into the Sacred Cauldron while the Chosen One burns in the Wicker of Sacrifice. This will allow Cernunnos to manifest as an avatar in the mortal world.

**Hearne References**: Multiple references to "Herne the Hunter" in medieval English texts, with Whitmore's notes connecting this figure to the Celtic Cernunnos. The name appears consistently in family records, folklore, and cult communications - clearly the key to unlocking the puzzle box.

---

## Opening the Puzzle Box

The bronze puzzle box requires the word "HEARNE" spelled across its seven rotating rings. Clues to this solution are scattered throughout the investigation:

- Whitmore's research consistently mentions "Hearne's Sacred Groves"
- Local folklore refers to "Herne the Hunter" as the leader of the Wild Hunt
- Family genealogies include references to "Hearne's chosen bloodline"
- Ogham translations mention "Hearne's blood-price" and "Hearne's return"

Once opened, the box contains:

- A copper scroll inscribed with Ogham script pointing to Maeshowe
- An ancient map showing ley line connections between Thornborough and Orkney
- A cryptic prophecy: "When Samhain's moon crowns the ancient stones, the blood of ages shall call the Horned One home"

This expanded Scene 1 provides multiple investigation paths while building atmosphere and establishing the adventure's central mystery. The basement clues create natural story progression while the genealogy and folklore elements set up the deeper conspiracy spanning centuries.
