# **"The Horn of the Wild Hunt"**

_A Complete Call of Cthulhu Adventure_

---

## **CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW**

**Time Period:** Modern Day (easily adaptable to 1920s)  
**Duration:** 4-6 sessions  
**Sanity Loss:** Moderate to Heavy (expect 10-25 points total)  
**Themes:** Ancient powers, nature vs. civilization, primal fear

---

## **THE CULT'S MOTIVATIONS**

### **The Antlered Brotherhood**

Founded in 1889 by Victorian occultist Lord Aldrich Blackwood, the Brotherhood believes modern civilization has severed humanity's connection to primal nature. They see Cernunnos not as a destroyer, but as a liberator who will tear down the "artificial constructs" of society.

**Core Beliefs:**

- Technology and urbanization are spiritual poison
- Humanity must return to its hunter-gatherer roots
- The Wild Hunt will cleanse the world of the "weak and civilized"
- Only those who embrace their primal nature will survive the new age

**Current Goal:** The autumn equinox of 2024 marks a rare celestial alignment that occurs every 400 years. Combined with the Horn of Cernunnos, they can permanently anchor the god in our reality, making him more than a seasonal manifestation.

---

## **KEY NPCs**

### **Dr. Margaret Whitmore** (Missing Curator)

- **Age:** 45, Celtic archaeology specialist
- **Background:** Brilliant but obsessive researcher who discovered the Horn's true nature
- **Current Status:** Kidnapped by the Brotherhood, held as the "Willing Sacrifice" (she's been psychologically broken and now believes in their cause)
- **Useful Knowledge:** Has translated key ritual texts, knows the exact timing requirements

### **Brother Gareth Thornfield** (Cult Leader)

- **Age:** 38, former environmental lawyer turned radical
- **Appearance:** Tall, gaunt, ritual scars across his chest, always wears antler pendant
- **Personality:** Charismatic but utterly ruthless, genuinely believes he's saving the world
- **Powers:** Can commune with spirits of the hunt, enhanced senses, resistance to cold/exhaustion
- **Goal:** Become Cernunnos's earthly avatar

### **Sister Moira Blackthorne** (High Priestess)

- **Age:** 52, descendant of the cult's founder
- **Background:** Inherited leadership but stepped aside for Thornfield's "vision"
- **Role:** Ritual specialist, keeper of ancient knowledge
- **Secret:** Growing doubts about the plan's wisdom, potential ally if approached correctly

### **Detective Inspector Sarah Chen** (Police Contact)

- **Age:** 34, London Metropolitan Police
- **Background:** Investigating the missing hikers, skeptical but thorough
- **Attitude:** Initially dismissive of occult theories, becomes believer after witnessing supernatural events
- **Resources:** Can provide police files, forensic evidence, backup (though regular officers are useless against supernatural threats)

### **Professor Edmund Blackwood** (Museum Contact)

- **Age:** 67, elderly descendant of cult founder
- **Background:** Rejected his family's legacy, became respectable academic
- **Knowledge:** Knows cult history, can translate ancient texts
- **Complication:** The cult wants him dead for his "betrayal"

## **DETAILED ACT BREAKDOWN**

## **ACT I: MUSEUM MYSTERIES**

### **Scene 1: The Call**

**Hook Options:**

- Investigators are researchers/academics consulting on the artifact
- Private investigators hired by Dr. Whitmore's worried sister
- Occult enthusiasts who read about the acquisition in academic journals
- Museum security consultants brought in after the incidents

### **Scene 2: Initial Investigation**

**The British Museum - Celtic Britain Gallery**

**What the Investigators Find:**

- **The Horn of Cernunnos:** Carved from ancient oak, bound with silver and strange metals, covered in ogham script
- **Dr. Whitmore's Notes:** Hidden in her desk, mention "The Eternal Hunt," references to Pliny and Caesar's accounts of druid rituals
- **Security Footage:** Shows shadowy figures that don't appear human, electronic equipment malfunctioning
- **Witness Accounts:** Night guard Jimmy Morrison describes hearing hunting horns and seeing "dogs the size of horses"

**Research Reveals:**

- Cernunnos was a Celtic god of the hunt, fertility, and the underworld
- The Wild Hunt is a European folk motif of ghostly hunters crossing the sky
- Similar artifacts have surfaced throughout history, always followed by disappearances
- The Horn was acquired from the estate of recently deceased Lord Pembroke

### **Scene 3: First Contact**

**The Supernatural Encounter**

As investigators research late into the evening, the museum transforms:

- Temperature drops dramatically
- Sounds of baying hounds echo through empty halls
- **Spectral Hounds** (use stats for hunting horror, but incorporeal) stalk the party
- The Horn begins to emit a low, resonant sound
- **Sanity Loss:** 1d4/1d8 for seeing the hounds, 1d6/1d10 if they attack

**Clues Discovered:**

- The Horn reacts to proximity of people with "hunter's instincts"
- Ogham inscriptions translate to "When the Horned One rides, the weak shall fall"
- Dr. Whitmore's final note: "They're coming for me. The Cotswolds. Avebury stones. The Brotherhood knows I have the key."

---

## **ACT II: THE MODERN DRUIDS**

### **Scene 4: Following the Trail**

**The Cotswolds Investigation**

**Avebury Village:**

- Locals are nervous, mention missing hikers (5 people in 3 weeks)
- **The Red Lion Pub:** Bartender Tom Ashford knows something but needs convincing
- **Local Police:** Constable Williams shows investigators missing person reports

**Missing Persons Pattern:**

- All were experienced hikers/outdoors enthusiasts
- Last seen near ancient stone circles
- Personal items found arranged in ritualistic patterns
- No bodies recovered

### **Scene 5: The Blackwood Estate**

**Abandoned Manor House (Cult Headquarters)**

**Exterior:**

- Gothic revival mansion, abandoned for decades
- Grounds show signs of recent activity
- Stone circle visible in rear gardens
- **Watchers:** 2-3 cultists on patrol (use Cultist stats, armed with hunting rifles)

**Interior Discoveries:**

- **Library:** Ancient texts on Celtic religion, modern ecological extremist literature
- **Ritual Chamber:** Basement converted to ceremonial space, walls covered in Celtic symbols
- **Dr. Whitmore's Prison:** Third floor bedroom, shows signs of psychological manipulation
- **The Plan:** Documents reveal the autumn equinox ritual, need for "seven pure hunters" as sacrifice

**Key Documents:**

- Letters between Thornfield and other cult cells across Britain
- Astronomical calculations for the ritual timing
- Dr. Whitmore's coerced translations of the ritual requirements

### **Scene 6: Confronting the Truth**

**The Stone Circle at Night**

If investigators arrive during a minor ritual:

- Witness the transformation of a willing cultist into a **Beast of the Wild Hunt**
- See spectral manifestations of Cernunnos (antlered figure, 12 feet tall)
- **Sanity Loss:** 1d6/1d12 for witnessing the transformation
- Combat with 4-6 cultists and 1 transformed beast
- Rescue opportunity for one of the kidnapped hikers

---

## **ACT III: THE WILD HUNT RISES**

### **Scene 7: The Gathering Storm**

**Preparing for the Final Ritual**

**Timeline Pressure:**

- Autumn equinox is in 48 hours
- Celestial alignment begins at sunset
- Ritual must be completed by midnight or wait another 400 years

**Investigator Options:**

- Seek help from Professor Blackwood for counter-ritual knowledge
- Contact Detective Chen for police backup
- Attempt to rescue Dr. Whitmore before the ceremony
- Sabotage cult preparations at the stone circle

### **Scene 8: The Wild Hunt**

**Final Confrontation at the Ancient Stone Circle**

**Setting the Scene:**

- Avebury stone circle transformed into nightmare landscape
- Reality warps as Cernunnos begins to manifest
- Weather turns violent: unnatural fog, temperature swings, aurora-like lights

**Phase 1: The Ritual Begins**

- 20+ cultists in attendance, chanting in ancient Celtic
- Dr. Whitmore bound at center altar, seemingly willing
- Thornfield begins transformation into avatar
- **Spectral Hunters** emerge from the stones

**Phase 2: Reality Breaks Down**

- Space becomes non-Euclidean around the circle
- Time flows differently in different areas
- **Cernunnos Manifests:** Partially materialized god-form
- **Sanity Loss:** 1d10/1d20 for seeing Cernunnos

**Phase 3: Stopping the Ritual**  
**Multiple Victory Conditions:**

- Destroy the Horn of Cernunnos (requires silver weapon, 15 hit points)
- Disrupt the ritual circle (move/destroy 3 of 7 key stones)
- Kill Thornfield before his transformation completes
- Convince Dr. Whitmore to reject her role (Psychology roll, -20% due to conditioning)

**Opposition:**

- **Brother Thornfield** (Transforming Avatar): Use Deep One Hybrid stats, add antlers (1d6+2 gore attack)
- **Sister Moira**: Potential ally if approached correctly
- **Spectral Hunters** (6): Use Byakhee stats but incorporeal
- **Cultists** (15): Standard cultist stats, fanatic morale
- **Manifestation of Cernunnos**: 200 HP, multiple attacks, reality-warping abilities

---

## **NASTY SURPRISES**

### **The Horn's Corruption**

Anyone who handles the Horn for extended periods begins to change:

- Enhanced senses but decreased social skills
- Violent dreams of hunting
- Gradual loss of empathy for "civilized" humans
- **Mechanic:** Each day of exposure requires a POW roll or lose 1 point permanently

### **The False Ally**

Professor Blackwood isn't as reformed as he appears:

- Secretly feeding information to the cult
- Plans to betray investigators at crucial moment
- Wants to claim leadership of the Brotherhood for himself
- **Reveal:** During Act III, he arrives with police backup that are actually cultists

### **The Viral Ritual**

The cult's true plan is more insidious:

- The ritual doesn't just summon Cernunnos once
- It creates a "spiritual virus" that spreads through modern communication
- Anyone who hears recordings or sees footage of the ritual begins transformation
- **Implication:** Investigators must prevent any media coverage of the final battle

### **The Willing Sacrifice Twist**

Dr. Whitmore isn't just psychologically broken:

- She's been partially possessed by an ancient druid priestess
- The possession makes her genuinely believe in the cause
- Killing her might complete the ritual rather than stop it
- **Solution:** Exorcism requires ancient Celtic counter-rituals

### **The Seasonal Cycle**

Even if stopped, Cernunnos isn't gone:

- The god exists in a seasonal cycle, weakest in summer, strongest in winter
- Destroying the Horn only delays his return
- Other artifacts exist around the world
- **Long-term Consequence:** Investigators have made enemies of a global network of eco-extremist cults

### **The Modern Wild Hunt**

If the ritual partially succeeds:

- Spectral hunters begin appearing worldwide
- They target "enemies of nature": industrialists, politicians, urban dwellers
- Each kill strengthens Cernunnos's hold on reality
- **Campaign Continuation:** Investigators must track down and stop the hunts globally

# **"The Horn of the Wild Hunt" - REVISED**

## **REVISED ACT III: THE WILD HUNT RISES**

### **Scene 7: The Gathering Storm** _(Enhanced)_

**The Wicker Man Revelation**

When investigators scout the ritual site 24 hours before the equinox, they discover the cult's most horrifying preparation:

**The Great Wicker Stag:**

- A massive wicker construction, 40 feet tall, shaped like a standing stag with enormous antlers
- Built in a clearing 200 yards from the main stone circle
- Contains **seven cages** woven into its structure - one for each "pure hunter" sacrifice
- Six cages already occupied by the missing hikers (still alive, but traumatized)
- The seventh, largest cage at the "heart" remains empty - reserved for Dr. Whitmore

**What Makes It Worse:**

- The wicker is interwoven with **human bones** from previous rituals
- **Living vines** have grown through the structure, pulsing with unnatural life
- The trapped victims report that the construct "whispers" to them at night
- **Sanity Loss:** 1d6/1d10 for seeing the Wicker Stag with its human cargo

---

## **ENHANCED CULT MOTIVATIONS**

### **The Wicker Stag Tradition**

The Antlered Brotherhood has elevated the ancient Celtic practice to new extremes:

**Historical Basis:**

- Caesar wrote of druids burning criminals in wicker effigies
- The Brotherhood believes the wicker man was actually a "vessel for divine transformation"
- They've discovered that burning sacrifices inside a properly consecrated wicker construct creates a "bridge" between worlds

**Modern Interpretation:**

- The seven hunters represent humanity's connection to primal instincts
- Their sacrifice will "feed" Cernunnos enough power to permanently manifest
- The Wicker Stag itself will become a permanent gateway for the Wild Hunt
- After the ritual, spectral hunters can emerge from any wooden structure

---

## **NEW KEY NPC**

### **Brother Marcus Ashwood** (The Wickerman)

- **Age:** 29, former art student turned cult craftsman
- **Background:** Obsessed with ancient construction techniques, built the Wicker Stag over three months
- **Personality:** Soft-spoken but utterly devoted, sees his creation as "living art"
- **Role:** Guards the Wicker Stag, performs daily "maintenance rituals" on the prisoners
- **Disturbing Detail:** Has begun incorporating parts of previous victims into new wicker constructs
- **Combat:** Uses a **Wicker Shield** (provides armor) and **Thorn Whip** (1d4+poison)

---

## **REVISED FINAL CONFRONTATION**

### **Scene 8: The Wild Hunt** _(Now with Wicker Man!)_

**The Ritual Sequence - Enhanced:**

**Phase 1: Lighting the Stag** _(New Opening)_

- Ritual begins with the lighting of the Wicker Stag at sunset
- Prisoners scream as flames begin at the base (they have 10 rounds before death)
- **Immediate Pressure:** Investigators must choose between stopping the main ritual or saving the victims
- **Sanity Loss:** 1d8/1d12 for hearing the screams, additional 1d4/1d8 each round they don't act

**Phase 2: The Burning Gateway**

- As the Wicker Stag burns, **Spectral Hunters** begin pouring out of the flames
- The construct doesn't collapse - it becomes a permanent gateway wreathed in green fire
- Dr. Whitmore is brought to the stone circle for the final phase
- **Tactical Challenge:** Investigators must fight enemies emerging from the burning stag while approaching the main ritual

**Phase 3: Avatar of the Hunt** _(Enhanced)_

- Thornfield begins his transformation in front of the blazing Wicker Stag
- Cernunnos manifests more powerfully due to the burning sacrifice
- The god's antlers mirror the stag's burning frame
- **Environmental Hazard:** Burning embers from the Wicker Stag ignite surrounding vegetation

---

## **ENHANCED VICTORY CONDITIONS**

**Multiple Objectives Create Difficult Choices:**

1. **Save the Prisoners:** Requires splitting party or abandoning ritual disruption
2. **Destroy the Horn:** Still necessary but now more dangerous due to burning chaos
3. **Collapse the Wicker Stag:**
    - Can be toppled with explosives or by destroying support struts
    - Closes the gateway but may kill remaining prisoners
    - Requires Engineering or Demolitions rolls
4. **Extinguish the Sacred Flames:**
    - Mundane water won't work - requires "pure" water (holy water, spring water, etc.)
    - Each gallon reduces the gateway's power
    - 20 gallons completely disrupts the connection

---

## **ENHANCED NASTY SURPRISES**

### **The Living Wicker**

The Wicker Stag isn't just a construct:

- **Surprise:** It's actually a **possessed entity** created from the tortured spirits of previous victims
- **Revelation:** Even if the fire is extinguished, the construct can **animate and attack**
- **Stats:** Use Giant Octopus stats but replace tentacles with "grasping wicker limbs"
- **Special:** Can grab and attempt to stuff investigators into its cage-body

### **The Incomplete Ritual Consequence**

If investigators save some but not all prisoners:

- **Partial Success:** Cernunnos manifests at reduced power
- **Ongoing Threat:** The surviving victims become **conduits** for the Wild Hunt
- **Twist:** They appear normal but periodically transform into spectral hunters
- **Long-term Problem:** They don't know they're possessed and return to normal lives

### **The Wicker Seed**

The most insidious surprise:

- **Hidden Truth:** Pieces of the original Wicker Stag were distributed to cult cells worldwide
- **Revelation:** Each fragment can grow into a new gateway given proper ritual
- **Implication:** Stopping this ritual only prevents the "Great Awakening" - smaller manifestations continue globally
- **Campaign Hook:** Investigators must track down 13 fragments across Britain and Europe

### **The Willing Prisoner**

Among the captured hikers:

- **Twist:** One prisoner, **Sarah Mills**, has been converted to the cause
- **Complication:** She sabotages rescue attempts and tries to prevent others from escaping
- **Moral Dilemma:** She's technically a victim but actively works against the investigators
- **Psychological Horror:** Her conversion shows how quickly "civilized" people can embrace primal brutality

### **The Burning Climax**

The final scene becomes a hellscape:

- **Visual:** 40-foot burning stag silhouetted against the night sky
- **Audio:** Crackling flames, screaming prisoners, chanting cultists, otherworldly hunting horns

- **Tactical Chaos:** Multiple fires, smoke obscuring vision, structural collapse threats

## **ALTERNATE WICKER MAN SCENARIOS**

### **If Investigators Arrive Early:**

- **Stealth Mission:** Infiltrate and weaken the structure before the ritual
- **Sabotage Options:** Remove key support beams, soak with water, free some prisoners quietly
- **Risk:** Getting caught means becoming the eighth sacrifice

### **If They Arrive Late:**

- **Horror Opening:** The Wicker Stag is already burning, screams fill the air
- **Desperate Rescue:** Racing against time as flames consume the structure
- **Moral Weight:** Some prisoners may already be dead, increasing stakes for survivors

### **If They Ignore It:**

- **Consequence:** The gateway opens fully, releasing a **permanent Wild Hunt**
- **Escalation:** Spectral hunters begin terrorizing the British countryside
- **Campaign Shift:** From stopping a ritual to containing an ongoing supernatural disaster

---

The addition of the Wicker Man transforms your climax into a truly memorable horror sequence that forces difficult choices and ramps up both the visual spectacle and moral complexity. The burning construct serves as both a ticking clock and a gateway to hell, perfectly embodying the Celtic tradition while adding modern psychological horror!

## **PRE-RITUAL COMPLICATIONS**

### **The Museum Heist**

**When:** Between Acts I and II  
**What Happens:** The cult infiltrates the British Museum to steal the Horn

- **Inside Job:** Security guard Jimmy Morrison is revealed as a cult sympathizer
- **The Theft:** Occurs during a "routine" fire drill that evacuates the building
- **Complication:** Investigators arrive to find the Horn gone and Morrison's body arranged in ritual fashion
- **New Pressure:** Must track the Horn to its destination while police suspect the investigators
- **Clue Trail:** Morrison's flat contains maps of the Cotswolds and a train ticket dated for tomorrow

### **The Academic Conspiracy**

**When:** During Act II research phase  
**What Happens:** Not all academics are helpful allies

- **Professor Blackwood's Colleagues:** Three other Celtic studies professors have been "consulting" on the project
- **The Revelation:** They're all cult members placed in universities decades ago
- **Active Opposition:** They provide false translations, misdirect research, and alert the cult to investigator activities
- **Paranoia Element:** Investigators can't trust any "expert" advice without verification
- **Combat Encounter:** Ambush at Oxford's Bodleian Library after hours

### **The Media Leak**

**When:** 48 hours before the ritual  
**What Happens:** Story breaks on social media and local news

- **The Leak:** Amateur photographer captures cult activity on smartphone
- **Viral Spread:** #WickerManCult trends on Twitter, attracts conspiracy theorists and thrill-seekers
- **Crowd Problem:** 50+ civilians descend on the area for the "show"
- **Police Response:** Increased security makes cult operations harder but also hampers investigators
- **Moral Dilemma:** Innocent bystanders will be caught in supernatural crossfire
- **Cult Adaptation:** Thornfield decides to use the crowd as additional sacrifices

## **PERSONAL COMPLICATIONS**

### **The Bloodline Curse**

**Target:** Any investigator with British ancestry  
**What Happens:** Ancient Celtic bloodlines carry supernatural significance

- **The Discovery:** Genealogy research reveals investigator descends from pre-Roman British tribes
- **The Effect:** Cernunnos can sense and influence them directly
- **Symptoms:** Vivid hunting dreams, compulsive behavior around the Horn, enhanced but uncontrolled senses
- **Mechanical Effect:** +20% to Track and Listen, -10% to all social skills, must make POW rolls to resist cult mental influence
- **Climax Complication:** During final ritual, affected investigator hears Cernunnos's direct commands

### **The Doppelganger**

**Target:** Investigator who handled the Horn most  
**What Happens:** Cult creates a supernatural duplicate

- **The Creation:** Using hair/blood left on the Horn, cult performs identity theft ritual
- **The Duplicate:** Perfect physical copy but with cult loyalties and hunting instincts
- **The Confusion:** Appears at investigator's home/work, ruins relationships and alibis
- **Investigation Chaos:** Police receive reports of investigator committing crimes they didn't commit
- **Final Twist:** Doppelganger appears during climax, forcing party to determine which is real

### **The Conversion**

**Target:** Investigator with highest Mythos knowledge  
**What Happens:** Knowledge of the truth begins changing them

- **Stage 1:** Dreams of running through forests, hunting prey
- **Stage 2:** Disgust with modern technology and urban environments
- **Stage 3:** Sympathy for cult's environmental message
- **Stage 4:** Active sabotage of party's efforts
- **Mechanical Progression:** Each Mythos point gained requires escalating POW rolls to resist
- **Reversal:** Only possible through specific counter-ritual requiring rare components

## **ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLICATIONS**

### **The Autumn Storm**

**When:** Final 24 hours  
**What Happens:** Supernatural weather system engulfs the Cotswolds

- **Manifestation:** Hurricane-force winds, torrential rain, unseasonable snow
- **Cause:** Cernunnos's influence bleeding through reality barriers
- **Effects:**
    - All movement reduced to half speed
    - Visibility limited to 10 yards
    - Electronic equipment fails intermittently
    - Driving becomes hazardous (Drive rolls every mile)
- **Tactical Impact:** Harder to coordinate with allies, easier for cult to move unseen
- **Atmospheric Bonus:** Creates perfect horror movie conditions

### **The Wild Hunt Scouts**

**When:** Throughout Act II and early Act III  
**What Happens:** Spectral hunters begin manifesting early

- **Harbingers:** Ghostly hounds track investigators across the countryside
- **Escalation:** Phantom riders appear on distant hilltops
- **Direct Contact:** Spectral arrows fired at investigators from empty fields
- **Psychological Warfare:** Hunting horns blow at 3 AM outside their lodgings
- **Sanity Erosion:** 1 point per night, no roll, representing accumulated stress
- **Mechanical Effect:** Investigators suffer from exhaustion (-10% to all skills after third night)

### **The Corrupted Landscape**

**When:** 12 hours before ritual  
**What Happens:** The countryside itself becomes hostile

- **Plant Growth:** Thorny vines grow at accelerated rates, blocking roads and paths
- **Animal Behavior:** Domestic animals flee the area, wild animals become aggressive
- **Geographic Distortion:** Familiar landmarks appear in wrong locations
- **Navigation Chaos:** GPS fails, compasses spin wildly, maps become unreliable
- **The Hunt's Domain:** Area around stone circle expands its influence hourly
- **Escape Routes:** Systematically cut off as ritual approaches

- **The Decision:** Thornfield opts for partial ritual rather than risk complete failure
- **The Effect:** Less powerful manifestation but immediate threat
- **The Chase:** Race across countryside as cult performs mobile ritual
- **The Climax:** Final confrontation on moving vehicles during supernatural storm

## **BETRAYAL COMPLICATIONS**

### **The Turned Ally**

**Who:** Detective Inspector Sarah Chen  
**When:** Just before final battle  
**What Happens:** Reveals she's been cult member all along

- **The Long Game:** Joined police specifically to monitor supernatural investigations
- **The Intelligence:** Has been feeding cult information about investigator activities
- **The Trap:** Leads investigators into ambush disguised as "police backup"
- **The Personal:** Genuinely likes investigators, shows hesitation at crucial moment
- **The Choice:** Investigators must decide whether to trust her apparent change of heart

### **The Desperate Academic**

**Who:** Professor Edmund Blackwood  
**When:** During Act III preparation  
**What Happens:** Makes deal with cult to save his own life

- **The Pressure:** Cult threatens his grandchildren
- **The Betrayal:** Provides false information about counter-ritual requirements

These complications can be mixed and matched based on your group's tolerance for chaos and the specific investigators in your campaign. ## **TECHNOLOGY AND COMMUNICATION CHANGES**

### **Investigation Methods**

**Research:**

- Library visits require appointments, limited hours
- No photocopying - must hand-copy or photograph documents
- Telegraph for long-distance communication (expensive, public)
- Telephone service limited, unreliable in rural areas

**Transportation:**

- Motor cars are luxury items, unreliable on country roads
- Train travel primary means of reaching Cotswolds
- Horse-drawn transport still common in rural areas
- Weather delays are serious impediments

**Documentation:**

- Photography requires bulky equipment, chemical processing
- Evidence must be sketched or described in detail
- No audio recording - witness testimony crucial
- Maps are hand-drawn or expensive surveyor products

### **Equipment Limitations**

**Weapons:**

- Revolvers and rifles standard, no automatic weapons
- Limited ammunition capacity
- Black powder still common in rural areas
- Military surplus from WWI available but illegal

**Lighting:**

- Oil lamps, candles, early flashlights with weak batteries
- Electric lighting rare outside cities
- Investigations after dark extremely challenging
- Fire is constant danger with open flames

**Medical:**

- Basic first aid only, no antibiotics
- Laudanum and alcohol for pain relief
- Mental health treatment primitive
- Serious injuries potentially fatal

## **SOCIAL AND CULTURAL ADAPTATIONS**

### **Class Distinctions**

**Upper Class (Lord Blackwood, cult leadership):**

- Enormous social power, above suspicion
- Access to private libraries, ancient documents
- Can command local police cooperation
- Traditional "right" to conduct estate business privately

**Middle Class (Academics, professionals):**

- Must navigate rigid social protocols
- Professional reputation everything
- Limited resources compared to aristocracy
- Vulnerable to upper-class pressure

**Working Class (Locals, constables):**

- Deeply deferential to "betters"
- Superstitious, more accepting of supernatural explanations
- Limited education but practical knowledge
- Loyal to local employers/landowners

### **Gender Roles - 1920s**

**Female Investigators:**

- Face constant skepticism and condescension
- Denied access to "men's spaces" (clubs, certain libraries)
- Cannot travel unaccompanied without social consequences
- Limited legal rights, especially regarding property

**Advantages:**

- Less suspected of dangerous activities
- Can access domestic servants' gossip networks
- "Feminine intuition" sometimes taken seriously
- Can use social expectations as camouflage

### **Post-War Trauma Context**

**Shell Shock Epidemic:**

- Many veterans suffer what we now know as PTSD
- No effective treatment available
- Supernatural experiences often dismissed as war trauma
- Creates both skepticism and vulnerability to cult recruitment

**Spiritualism Boom:**

- Séances and mediums extremely popular
- Families desperate to contact war dead
- Occult practices more socially acceptable
- Investigators might be mistaken for spiritualists
