### Location and Discovery 
**Where:** Hidden cellar beneath Blackwood Estate's kitchen, behind a false wall 
**Access:** Requires **Spot Hidden** roll or following the metallic, salty odor mixed with decay and ozone 
**Atmosphere:** Temperature 20 degrees colder than rest of house, breath visible, single oil lamp burning with greenish flame that never consumes oil 

### Physical Description 
The cellar opens into a circular chamber carved from living rock. At the center stands an enormous iron cauldron, six feet across, suspended over a cold fire pit by a complex pulley system. The medieval ironwork shows intricate Celtic knotwork designs, while the pulley mechanism demonstrates sophisticated engineering knowledge. 
**The Cauldron Contents:** - Coarse salt mixed with dried herbs (rosemary, sage, rowan, and unidentifiable components) - Salt crystals stained pink-brown with old blood, shifting and moving as if alive - Iron filings from ancient battle weapons ground into the mixture - Bone fragments from at least twelve previous victims spanning centuries - Faint luminescence suggesting supernatural energy storage 
### The Preservation Process 
**The Ritual Method:** 
- Victims suspended head-down over the cauldron via pulley system 
- Throat cut with precise surgical technique using ceremonial bronze knife 
- Blood drained in exactly one-pint sessions over 28-day lunar cycle 
- Body packed in enchanted salt mixture between drainage sessions 
- Victims remain conscious but paralyzed throughout the process 

**The Living Death State:** 
- Heartbeat barely detectable, breathing once every 30 seconds 
- Eyes track movement, pupils respond to light 
- Full consciousness trapped in unresponsive body 
- Constant, excruciating pain with no ability to communicate 
- Any attempt to move the victim causes agonizing convulsions 
### The Champion Creation Legend 
**Ancient Celtic Belief:** Warriors who die in sacred salt return as **Fianna** - legendary champions who serve as eternal guardians of the god's earthly domain. The process transforms exceptional hunters, soldiers, and athletes into supernatural protectors who rise at the full moon following Cernunnos's manifestation. 
**Historical Pattern:** The cult has performed this ritual for over 400 years, creating champions during each major historical crisis. Each preserved warrior represents a different era, with their weapons and armor integrated into their undead flesh through the salt preservation process. 
### Current Victim - Thomas Brennan 
**Status:** Day 21 of 28-day preservation cycle 
**Condition:** Alive but dying, throat wound fatal yet somehow breathing 
**Consciousness:** Fully aware, eyes fill with tears when investigators approach 
**Communication:** Mouth moves soundlessly trying to form words - careful observation reveals he's mouthing "KILL ME" 
**Investigation:** **Medicine Roll** reveals impossible survival and constant agony 
### The Historical Champions 
Bone fragments and artifacts in the cauldron reveal centuries of victims, each selected for exceptional physical prowess and survival instincts. Personal effects buried in salt layers show the cult has been active since at least the 14th century. 
### Supernatural Effects 
**Environmental Anomalies:** 
- Air around cauldron resists movement, feels thick and electric 
- Electronic equipment malfunctions within 20 feet 
- Watches stop, compasses spin wildly 
- Faint voices in ancient Celtic heard with **Listen** rolls 
- Salt forms Celtic knot designs spontaneously when not observed 
**The Living Nightmare:** 
- Pulley system creaks even when untouched 
- Phantom dripping sounds continue regardless of examination 
- Salt crystals shift and realign when not directly watched 
- Under certain lighting, the salt mixture pulses with inner light 
### Moral Dilemmas 
**The Mercy Question:** Thomas Brennan clearly suffers but remains technically alive. Killing him could constitute murder in 1920s law, yet leaving him condemns him to continued agony. The investigators must weigh legal, moral, and practical considerations while time runs short.
**Interference Consequences:** 
- **Removing Thomas:** May trigger mass resurrection of all preserved victims 
- **Destroying Cauldron:** Could release current champions or kill all victims
- **Disrupting Process:** Might cause all preserved victims to animate immediately 
- **Consecrating Space:** Requires specific religious rituals investigators may not know 
### Investigation Discoveries 
**Forensic Evidence:** 
- Multiple drainage cycles show systematic, precise bleeding 
- Each session drains exactly one pint - no more, no less 
- Timeline reveals process takes exactly 28 days (one lunar cycle) 
- Recent rope wear suggests frequent use of pulley system 
**Ritual Components:** 
- **Sacred Salt:** Mined from ancient Celtic sites, blessed under previous full moons 
- **Preservation Herbs:** Rosemary (remembrance), sage (wisdom), rowan (protection) 
- **Battle Iron:** Filings from weapons used in historical conflicts 
- **Champion Bone:** Ground remains of previous warriors from centuries past 
**Historical Depth:** 
- Some remains show medieval metalwork integrated into bones 
- Others contain Bronze Age artifacts fused with skeletal structure 
- Personal effects reveal victims from across British history 
- All show evidence of identical preservation process 
### Sanity Loss 
**Initial Discovery:** 1d8/1d12 for seeing the suspended victim and ritual setup 
**Closer Examination:** Additional 1d6/1d8 for realizing Thomas is still alive and aware 
**Realizing the Scope:** 1d4/1d6 for understanding centuries of victims 
**The Impossible Survival:** 1d10/1d12 if investigators realize victims are conscious during preservation 
### Combat Encounters 
**Premature Resurrection:** If investigators interfere with the process, all preserved victims may animate simultaneously. Six previous champions rise from the salt in various states of preservation, retaining mortal skills but gaining supernatural resilience. 
**Guardian Spirits:** The cauldron is protected by spectral manifestations of previous victims who "welcome" new arrivals. These whispered voices in ancient Celtic can manifest as incorporeal attackers if the site is threatened. 
### Campaign Implications 
**If Thomas Completes the Process:** At the next full moon, he rises as a perfected warrior with doubled physical stats, supernatural tracking abilities, and immunity to fear. Tragically, he retains his memories and personality but cannot disobey Cernunnos, creating a recurring antagonist who remembers the investigators who tried to save him. 
**The Ancient Army:** Cernunnos's manifestation calls all champions regardless of preparation state, potentially awakening centuries of preserved warriors from hidden sites across Britain. Each represents a different historical threat with period-appropriate weapons and tactics integrated into their undead forms.
